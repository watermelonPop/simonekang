From an early age, I recognized the power of education and the joy of helping others learn. My journey as a tutor started at the age of 12, and I dedicated my time and skills to support students in math, computer science, and speech and debate. I continued volunteering until I was 18, and then began working as a tutor in those same subjects. I specialized in these subjects for middle school and elementary school-aged children. I’m passionate about helping cultivate young minds, especially for subjects like math and public speaking. I’m committed to this cause, and I firmly believe that early engagement with these challenging areas is pivotal for children’s holistic development. In fact, American University wrote that “STEM education has been shown to be a predictor of future academic achievement. For example, a study by researchers at the University of California Irvine found that early math skills were the most consistently predictive measure of future academic success among kindergarten to fifth grade students”(American University, 2020). Those ‘aha’ moments where everything just clicks for my students is so genuinely rewarding and satisfying, and confirms my love and dedication to these children.
